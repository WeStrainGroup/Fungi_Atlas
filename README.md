# The Human Gut Mycobiome Atlas

## Summary

Fungi remain understudied in the human gut owing to marker-specific analytical challenges and fragmented datasets. Here, we developed MycoGAP, a gut-focused, ITS-aware workflow for standardized analysis of heterogeneous datasets. Using MycoGAP, we constructed the Human Gut Mycobiome Atlas from over 46,400 samples spanning diverse populations and linked to harmonized metadata, yielding 3.1 billion microfungal sequences. The Atlas revealed substantial non-microfungal signal and putative novelty: only 57.6% of ITS reads were assigned to microfungi, while 54.2% of microfungi taxa lacked close UNITE matches and accounted for 18.8% of reads. Technical and geographic factors were major sources of variation, with biogeographic signatures spanning taxon abundance, co-abundance networks, and intraspecies variation. Across 23 case-control cohorts, the Atlas mapped disease-associated fungal variation and identified context-specific biomarker candidates. Together, MycoGAP and the Atlas provide a standardized framework and global resource for understanding gut fungal ecology and its relevance to human health.

This repository contains the code used to validate and benchmark MycoGAP, construct the Human Gut Mycobiome Atlas, and perform the downstream analyses reported in the manuscript.

### 01 - MycoGAP benchmark

This directory implements construction of simulated fungal mock communities and validation and benchmarking of MycoGAP using simulated mock communities. Script 14 implements the classifier audit described under identification of analytical challenges in human gut ITS metabarcoding.

| Order | Script and description |
|---:|---|

| 02 | **[`02_validate_its2_regions.sh`](scripts/01_mycogap_benchmark/02_validate_its2_regions.sh)**<br>**Purpose:** Validates candidate fungal amplicons with ITSx fungal HMMs and extracts their ITS2 regions.<br>**Inputs:** Candidate amplicon FASTA from step 01.<br>**Outputs:** ITSx sequences, tables, and detailed results.<br>**Methods/figures:** Construction of simulated fungal mock communities. |

|---:|---|
| 01 | **[`01_analyze_intraspecies_divergence.R`](scripts/08_intraspecies_divergence/01_analyze_intraspecies_divergence.R)**<br>**Purpose:** Merges and filters unclustered ASVs, prepares separate ITS1/ITS2 FASTAs, organizes global SHs, selects 67 SHs using ASV count and prevalence, identifies each sample's dominant ASV, integrates tree/Mantel results, and creates the analysis plots.<br>**Inputs:** Project-level unclustered phyloseq objects, metadata, VSEARCH outputs, and tree/Mantel outputs.<br>**Outputs:** Global SH tables/FASTA, selected SH list, dominant-ASV table, Mantel summary, and Figure 5 plot files.<br>**Methods/figures:** Geographic association with microbial phylogeny; Figure 5B-E. |
| 02 | **[`02_cluster_global_shs.sh`](scripts/08_intraspecies_divergence/02_cluster_global_shs.sh)**<br>**Purpose:** Clusters pooled ITS1 and ITS2 ASVs separately at 98.5% identity with VSEARCH to define cross-project global SHs.<br>**Inputs:** ITS1/ITS2 FASTAs written by step 01.<br>**Outputs:** Centroid FASTAs and `.uc` cluster mappings.<br>**Methods/figures:** Geographic association with microbial phylogeny; Figure 5B-E inputs. |
| 03 | **[`03_build_sh_phylogenies.sh`](scripts/08_intraspecies_divergence/03_build_sh_phylogenies.sh)**<br>**Purpose:** Aligns ASVs within each selected SH with MUSCLE and infers IQ-TREE phylogenies using ModelFinder Plus and 1,000 ultrafast bootstrap replicates.<br>**Inputs:** Per-SH FASTAs and selected SH list from step 01.<br>**Outputs:** Per-SH alignments and IQ-TREE files.<br>**Methods/figures:** Geographic association with microbial phylogeny; Figure 5E inputs. |
| 04 | **[`04_run_mantel_tests.R`](scripts/08_intraspecies_divergence/04_run_mantel_tests.R)**<br>**Purpose:** Extracts cophenetic distances from SH trees, maps dominant ASVs to samples, aligns genetic and geographic matrices, and performs Pearson Mantel tests with 999 permutations; step 01 applies the FDR correction when integrating the results.<br>**Inputs:** SH trees, dominant-ASV mappings, sample metadata, and geographic distances.<br>**Outputs:** `mantel_result.csv`.<br>**Methods/figures:** Geographic association with microbial phylogeny; Figure 5E. |

### 09 - Disease variation

This directory implements alpha-diversity and beta-diversity analysis of case-control cohorts, differential abundance analysis, and predictive modelling for disease states.

| Order | Script and description |
|---:|---|
| 01 | **[`01_analyze_disease_variation.R`](scripts/09_disease_variation/01_analyze_disease_variation.R)**<br>**Purpose:** Analyzes 23 case-control cohorts using two-sided Wilcoxon tests with Hodges-Lehmann estimates and 95% CIs, robust Aitchison PERMANOVA with 999 permutations and PCoA, and genus/SH ANCOM-BC2 (prevalence >0.1, absolute log2 fold change >1, FDR <0.05); it then evaluates 300-tree Random Forest and LASSO classifiers by LOOCV with DeLong 95% AUC CIs.<br>**Inputs:** Curated cohort metadata; Atlas genus profiles; global-SH profiles.<br>**Outputs:** Alpha/beta tables, PCoA coordinates, ANCOM-BC2 results, and prediction-performance tables.<br>**Methods/figures:** Alpha-diversity and beta-diversity analysis of case-control cohorts, differential abundance analysis, and predictive modelling for disease states; Figure 6A-C and Figure S9A-C. |
| 02 | **[`02_plot_disease_variation.R`](scripts/09_disease_variation/02_plot_disease_variation.R)**<br>**Purpose:** Plots the 23 cohort sample counts, case-minus-control alpha-diversity estimates, genus/SH PERMANOVA effects and PCoA, ANCOM-BC2 results, and LOOCV classifier AUCs, then assembles the main disease figure.<br>**Inputs:** All tables from step 01.<br>**Outputs:** `pcoa_plot_genus.pdf`, `pcoa_plot_sh.pdf`, and `final_plot.pdf`.<br>**Methods/figures:** Figure 6A-C and Figure S9A-C. |
